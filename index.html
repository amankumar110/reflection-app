Refraction of light

The bending effect of light when it travels from one medium to another medium.

when light travels from denser medium to rare medium(glass to Air) it bends away from normal.

when light travels from rare to denser medium(air to glass) it bends towards the normal



Important Terms

1.Incident ray

The Ray Which falls On the surface of another medium and refracted inside the medium

2.Refracted Ray

when incident Ray falls on the surface of another it deviates it's path inside the medium and that deviated ray is called reflected ray.

Angle of incidence

the angle at which incident ray falls on the surface

OR

The angle between Normal and Incident day

Angle of Reflection

The angle at which incident ray is reflected

OR

The angle between normal and reflected Ray

Normal ray Or Normal

The perpendicular angle between angle of incidence and angle of Reflection

Laws of Refraction

1st Law Of Refraction

This law States that the incident ray,Refracted ray and Normal must lie on same plane.

2nd Law Of Refraction

The Ratio Between Sine Angle Of incidence and Sine angle Of Refraction Is Called Refractive index.It is diffrent for diffrent media and determines how much light will be refracted from the medium.

It is represented by "n"

n=Sin Angle of Incidence / Sin Angle of Reflection

This is also called Snell's Law

for instance,Diamond has highest refractive index between all other media so it is most sparkling substance and its refractive index is 2.42
